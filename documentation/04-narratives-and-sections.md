# Narratives and Sections

The narrative blocks communicate our perspective on enterprise adoption. They explain why we start with broad access, how usage reveals real needs, how we harden what works, and how education transfers capability. These narratives are paired with shelves so the story is always grounded in real catalog examples.

Current phases and titles:

1) Access Over Abstraction
- Message: Get the complete toolkit on Day 1, not a roadmap.
- Purpose: Replace speculative planning with immediate access and evidence.

2) Friction as Signal (Z-layout)
- Message: Start Imperfect. Optimize Later.
- Purpose: Show that friction and adoption are the signal for what to harden.

3) From Experiments to Infrastructure
- Message: Don't build a custom fortress, before knowing what you need.
- Purpose: Position custom development as a later, optional phase driven by data.

4) The Knowledge Transfer Protocol (Z-layout)
- Message: Active Education and Training in Enterprise AI.
- Purpose: Make education, training, and capability transfer explicit and central.

5) Organic Integration
- Message: Build in-house competence instead of permanent reliance.
- Purpose: Emphasize ownership and long-term internal capability.

Supporting shelf strategy:
- Use curated shelves as evidence for each narrative, not as raw inventory.
- Combine kinetic elements (ticker, tabbed stacks, drag crate) with fewer static shelves to keep density without endless scrolling.

Together, the narratives and shelves form a marketing page that feels like a product catalog while still delivering the enterprise consulting message.
